What it is

Study Planner is a lightweight web app that helps you break subjects into smaller tasks, track confidence, and plan focused sessions. It’s designed to stay fast and distraction-free: no feeds, no infinite scroll, just a clean board, a daily focus list, and a schedule view that makes timeboxing simple.

You can use it like a personal “study cockpit”: keep an overview of what matters, decide what to do next, then commit to a small session. Over time, the confidence meter and your completed focus items make it easier to see what’s improving and where you should spend your next hour.

How your data is stored

By default, your study data is stored locally in your browser (using localStorage). This means your subjects, tasks, notes, and planning information stay on your device and remain available even when the server is offline. After your first successful visit, the app can also be used offline thanks to its service worker.

If you choose to create an account, you can enable cloud sync. In that mode, the app uploads your Study Planner state so you can restore it on another device. The server keeps recent versions as backups to help you recover if something goes wrong during syncing.
